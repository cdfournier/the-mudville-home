About

  • 2bedrooms

  • 2,386sq. ft

The perfect blend of modern amenities in a country setting and originally constructed around 1910, The Mudville Home was completely restored, remodeled, and updated in 2004.

The house features 2 bedrooms and 2.5 baths and is listed as 2,386 square feet of living space nestled on 0.24 acres.

Customized features include a professional cook's kitchen with custom cabinets (including a built-in wine rack) designed and built by Kochman, Reidt + Haigh, a Sub-Zero unit with extra built-in refrigerator drawers, Wolf gas range with stainless steel hood, Miele dishwasher, and Silestone counter-tops including a spacious breakfast bar. The house and hot water are heated by an energy-efficient Buderus Logamax Plus GB142 boiler and also features a complete security system including motion, temperature and water monitor devices.

The spacious open-concept first floor features ebony-stained oak floors, a living room surrounded by windows with a cozy gas fireplace from Vermont Castings, and an entry foyer with a custom built-in bookcase. The second floor features two bedrooms including a spacious master bedroom, study/dressing room, and remote-controlled Velux skylights (with automatic rain sensors). The master bath features a spa shower with two large rainwater shower heads and a marble floor.

Designed by landscape architect David Hawk (designer of the grounds at the Wequassett Inn in Chatham, MA), The Mudville Home landscaping features a brand new Azek deck, a natural gas barbecue (never refill a propane tank again), and backyard with mature trees, stone wall, fieldstone patio, and irrigation system. The yard also abuts a protected wetlands area.

Location

The Mudville Home is located only a short walk from the center of town, and the home is also within easy walking distance of Lake Winthrop and Goodwill Park.

The Town of Holliston is located 21.6 miles west of Boston and is within short driving distance of Interstate 495 and the Mass Pike (part of Interstate 90).

Holliston was first settled in 1659 by Massachusetts Bay Puritans and was incorporated on December 3, 1724. As of 2014, Holliston's population is 13,668 people. Holliston public schools spend $17,343 per student. The average school expenditure in the U.S. is $12,435. There are about 12.7 students per teacher in Holliston.

87.57% of the Holliston workforce is employed in white-collar jobs, well above the national average. Overall, Holliston is a town of professionals, managers and sales and office workers. There are a lot of people living in Holliston who work in management occupations (17.68%), sales jobs (13.61%) and teaching (9.43%). Holliston also has more residents who work in computers and math than 95% of the places in the US.

Holliston is a great place for families with children. First of all, many other families with children live here, making Holliston a place where both parents and children are more likely to develop social ties with other families, as well as find family-oriented services and community.

The town's good public school district and large population of college-educated adults provide an environment conducive to academic values. With regard to real estate, Holliston has a high rate of owner-occupied single family homes, which tends to reflect stability in the local community.

Finally, Holliston's overall crime rate ranks among the lowest in the country, making it one of the safest places to raise a family.
